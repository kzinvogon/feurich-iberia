Brands we advise on

Impartial across every leading maker

We are not a dealer for any single marque. We assess each maker — and each individual instrument — on tone, touch, build, value and resale, then recommend what genuinely fits you. Below is a guide to the brands we most often advise on, new and pre-owned.

Featured partner

Feurich — Vienna, since 1851

Feurich pairs a long Viennese heritage with genuinely modern engineering: expressive uprights and grands, all-RAL bespoke finishes, and invisibly integrated self-playing systems. As our featured partner we know the range intimately — and we will still tell you honestly when another maker suits you better.

The wider market

Other makers we regularly recommend

Hamburg / New York Steinway & Sons

The concert-grand benchmark, with unrivalled brand recognition and resale strength. We help you judge whether a new, restored or vintage Steinway is the right call.

Vienna Bösendorfer

Warm, complex Viennese tone and the extended-bass Imperial. A distinctive voice for players who want colour over sheer power.

Sacile, Italy Fazioli

Modern, hand-built grands celebrated for clarity, dynamic range and exceptional craftsmanship. Limited production, exceptional instruments.

Japan Yamaha

Superb consistency and value at every level — often the ideal first serious piano, and a benchmark for reliability.

Japan Kawai · Shigeru Kawai

Refined touch and tone, with the hand-built Shigeru line competing with Europe's finest at strong value.

Leipzig Blüthner

The famous singing, romantic tone from the Aliquot fourth string — a favourite for lyrical repertoire.

Berlin C. Bechstein

Clear, articulate German voicing with a strong tradition — excellent new and as carefully restored vintage.

Germany Schimmel · Grotrian

Respected German makers offering beautifully engineered instruments across a range of budgets.
